Nine Lives. One True Love

I Died Nine Times. Love Brought Me back.
My father survived a Japanese prison camp. I survived the jungles of New Guinea and Borneo, open-heart surgery, liver cancer, a liver transplant, depression, and more close calls than I can count. I buried dreams, damaged relationships, and often got in my own way. This is the story of a jungle boy who should have died many times—but didn’t. More importantly, it is the story of how faith, forgiveness, and one remarkable woman taught me that surviving is not the same as living—and that one true love can arrive even after a lifetime of wrong turns.
Why This Book Matters
Inside these pages you’ll discover: • A father who survived a Japanese prison camp during World War II • A childhood spent in the jungles of New Guinea and Borneo • Poisoned arrows, exploding bridges, and tribal warfare • Four marriages, painful mistakes, and one true love • Open-heart surgery, liver cancer, and a liver transplant • A battle with depression that nearly ended everything • Unexpected friendships with celebrities, world-class leaders, and music icon Eric Clapton • Encounters that brought me from jungle villages to boardrooms connected to Donald Trump • The extraordinary journey from missionary kid to minister, CEO, transplant survivor, and yacht entrepreneur

About the Author
Lester Keizer has lived in six countries and worn many hats: missionary kid, pastor, healthcare executive, technology entrepreneur, transplant survivor, and yacht charter owner. Today he lives in La Paz, Mexico with his wife Fabiola, grateful for every sunrise, every second chance, and every day of gifted time.
